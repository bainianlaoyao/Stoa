## Context Report: Whether `Stoa` Should Be Renamed to `OpenCodex` or `openxxx`

### Why This Was Gathered
Support a naming decision for the project's public brand and X/GitHub promotion strategy.

### Summary
`OpenCodex` is not a good rename. It is already occupied in the market, strongly collides with OpenAI's actively expanding `Codex` product brand, and narrows the product perception toward a single provider even though this repository is explicitly multi-provider.

More broadly, `openxxx` is a weak naming pattern for a primary brand. It is easy to understand, but it is less ownable, less distinctive, and more likely to blend into a crowded open-source/AI tool landscape. If a rename is pursued, the better direction is a more distinctive suggestive/arbitrary brand plus a clear descriptive tagline.

### Key Findings
- `Codex` is an active OpenAI product brand, not a dormant historical label.
- `OpenCodex` is already used by multiple live products and open-source repos.
- Your product is not Codex-only; the repo positions it as a local multi-provider workspace for Codex, Claude, OpenCode, and other CLI agents.
- `openxxx` names are structurally weak because descriptive/generic naming is harder to protect and usually less distinctive.
- `Stoa` itself is also not a uniquely owned search term in adjacent AI/software spaces, so "keep current name forever" is not obviously correct either.

### Risks / Unknowns
- [!] This is not a formal legal trademark clearance. A real rename should still include a proper trademark and domain screening pass in the jurisdictions you care about.
- [!] `Stoa` also has meaningful naming collisions in adjacent AI/software markets, including `withstoa.com`, `onstoa.com`, and `stoafactory.dev`, so keeping `Stoa` is not automatically optimal either.
- [?] The decisive factor is not just legal/name availability, but whether your target users respond better to a differentiated umbrella brand or a more descriptive tool name.
